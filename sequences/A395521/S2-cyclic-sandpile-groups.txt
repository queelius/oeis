NAME (one line):
Number of connected graphs on n vertices whose sandpile group K(G) is
cyclic.

DATA (offset 2):
1, 2, 5, 14, 69, 520, 7075

OFFSET:
2,2

COMMENTS:
The sandpile group (also known as critical group, Jacobian, or Picard
group) of a connected graph G is K(G) = Z^(n-1) / image(L'), where L'
is the reduced Laplacian. |K(G)| equals the number of spanning trees
of G.

A graph G has cyclic sandpile group iff the Smith Normal Form of L'
has all but possibly the last invariant factor equal to 1. The trivial
group K(G) = Z/1 is included; a graph has trivial K(G) iff G is a tree
(A000055).

a(n) - A000055(n) counts the connected graphs on n vertices with
non-trivial cyclic sandpile group K(G) = Z/k for some k >= 2:

  n:                    2  3  4  5   6   7    8
  a(n):                 1  2  5 14  69 520 7075
  A000055(n):           1  1  2  3   6  11   23
  non-trivial cyclic:   0  1  3 11  63 509 7052

REFERENCES:
N. L. Biggs, Chip-firing and the critical group of a graph,
Journal of Algebraic Combinatorics, 9 (1999), 25-45.

LINKS:
A. Towell, Catalog of sandpile groups for connected graphs on n <= 8
vertices, Apr 2026 (project repository).

CROSSREFS:
A001349 (all connected graphs on n vertices)
A000055 (trees, with K(G) trivial)

EXAMPLE:
n=4: of 6 connected graphs, 5 have cyclic K(G):
  2 trees (trivial), the 3-cycle plus pendant (Z/3), 4-cycle (Z/4),
  4-cycle plus chord (Z/8). The complete graph K_4 has K(K_4) = (Z/4)^2
  which is non-cyclic, so it is excluded. a(4) = 5.
