NAME (one line):
Number of non-isomorphic abelian groups appearing as the sandpile group
K(G) of a connected graph on n vertices.

DATA (offset 2):
1, 2, 5, 16, 71, 461, 4855

OFFSET:
2,2

COMMENTS:
The sandpile group (also known as critical group, Jacobian, or Picard
group) of a connected graph G is K(G) = Z^(n-1) / image(L'), where L'
is the reduced Laplacian (the Laplacian of G with one row and column
removed; the choice of row/column does not affect the group up to
isomorphism). |K(G)| equals the number of spanning trees of G by the
matrix-tree theorem.

This sequence counts the number of distinct (up to group isomorphism)
abelian groups arising as K(G) over all connected graphs G on n
vertices.

The number of connected graphs on n vertices is A001349; many of these
share the same sandpile group, so a(n) <= A001349(n).

EXAMPLE:
n=4: 6 connected graphs on 4 vertices, with sandpile groups
  trivial (the 2 trees), Z/3, Z/4, Z/8, Z/16. So 5 distinct groups.

Verification
================================================================

The catalog is exhaustive: every non-isomorphic connected graph on
n=2..8 vertices is enumerated via pynauty, the reduced Laplacian is
formed, and Smith Normal Form computes the invariant factors of K(G).

Cross-check: the number of graphs at each n matches A001349 exactly
(1, 2, 6, 21, 112, 853, 11117); the trivial-group count matches A000055
(1, 1, 2, 3, 6, 11, 23); the maximum |K(G)| matches Cayley's formula
n^(n-2) at K_n.

Frontier: n=8 reached in seconds; n=9 has 261080 connected graphs and
is feasible; n=10 has 11716571 graphs, feasible with care. Beyond
n=10 starts to require infrastructure beyond simple loops.
