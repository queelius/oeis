OEIS submission: T(c, g) = number of prime knots with crossing number c and genus g

NAME
Triangle read by rows: T(c, g) is the number of prime knots with crossing number c and three-genus g, for c >= 3 and 1 <= g <= floor((c-1)/2).

DATA
1, 1, 1, 1, 1, 2, 2, 4, 1, 2, 10, 9, 4, 22, 22, 1, 2, 44, 93, 26

OFFSET
3,6

COMMENTS
The genus of a knot is the minimum genus of an orientable Seifert surface bounded by the knot. For prime knots through 10 crossings the genus is bounded by floor((c-1)/2), with equality achieved by the (2, 2k+1) torus knots and a small number of others.

Row sums give A002863, the number of prime knots with c crossings: 1, 1, 2, 3, 7, 21, 49, 165 for c = 3..10.

Column g = 1 (genus-1 prime knots, A002863-style refinement): 1, 1, 1, 1, 2, 2, 4, 2 for c = 3..10. (The drop at c = 10 from 4 to 2 is genuine.)

The Perko duplicate 10_161 = 10_162 is excluded.

The table truncates at c = 10 because at c = 11 the genus distribution depends sensitively on enumeration conventions for chiral knots; see the linked source code for discussion.

LINKS
Alex Towell, <a href="https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.19962842">Joint distributions of knot invariants for prime knots through 11 crossings</a>, Zenodo, 2026.

EXAMPLE
Triangle begins:
  c=3:  1
  c=4:  1
  c=5:  1, 1
  c=6:  1, 2
  c=7:  2, 4, 1
  c=8:  2, 10, 9
  c=9:  4, 22, 22, 1
  c=10: 2, 44, 93, 26

Row 7: of the 7 prime knots with 7 crossings, 2 have genus 1 (7_2, 7_4), 4 have genus 2 (7_3, 7_5, 7_6, 7_7), and 1 has genus 3 (the (2,7) torus knot 7_1).
